Bok Choy Tofu Stir Fry

Ready in just 30 minutes, this easy vegan dinner is packed with veggies and plant-based protein. Enjoy this bok choy tofu stir fry with rice, noodles, or a side of roasted vegetables.

With ingredients like bok choy, red bell pepper, and shiitake mushrooms, this recipe is teeming with veggies. You can also add other vegetables like sliced carrots or chopped broccoli.

To make this vegan stir fry recipe a full meal, enjoy it with rice or noodles. You can also serve it with chopped fresh scallions and a side of Roasted Sugar Snap Peas.

Ingredients

Crispy Tofu

  • 14 ounces extra-firm tofu pressed and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 3 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce or tamari
  • ¼ teaspoon chili flakes optional
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 6 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 3 tablespoons cold water

Bok Choy Tofu Stir Fry

  • 1 bell pepper deseeded and diced
  • 4 ounces shiitake mushrooms sliced
  • 3 ½ cups bok choy baby or large, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic minced
  • Scallions chopped, for serving
  • Sesame seeds
  • Oil

Instructions

Crispy Tofu

  1. In a shallow bowl, combine the marinade ingredients.
  2. Toss the cubes of tofu in the marinade.
  3. Sprinkle the cornstarch onto a plate. Using a slotted spoon or tongs, transfer the tofu onto the plate and toss until coated.
  4. Warm 1 tablespoon oil in a large skillet. Add the tofu and cook until crispy, about 1 minute on each side. Once cooked, place the tofu on a plate lined with a paper towel. Set aside.

Bok Choy Tofu Stir Fry

  1. To the pan, warm 2 tablespoons oil. Add the bell pepper, shiitake mushrooms, and sliced bok choy stems. Cook for 5 minutes or until tender. Add the garlic and cook for about 30 seconds.
  2. Add the bok choy leaves and cook until wilted, about 3 minutes.
  3. While the bok choy leaves are cooking, combine the cornstarch and 3 tablespoons cold water. Stir well.
  4. To the pan, add the cornstarch mixture, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Stir well, then add the tofu and heat until warmed through, about 1 minute.
  5. Serve with rice, noodles, or on its own. Top with chopped scallions and sesame seeds, and enjoy warm.

Try adding other veggies like diced carrots, chopped broccoli, or sliced zucchini. For more nutrition and flavor, add a handful of spinach or extra bok choy leaves.
